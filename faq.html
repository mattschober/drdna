What is disaster recovery and why do I need it? ▼

Disaster recovery (DR) is a comprehensive strategy and set of technologies that enable your organization to quickly recover IT systems, applications, and data after unexpected disruptions. These disruptions can include natural disasters, cyberattacks, hardware failures, human error, or power outages.

Without proper disaster recovery, businesses face:

  • Extended downtime leading to lost revenue
  • Permanent data loss affecting operations
  • Damage to customer trust and reputation
  • Regulatory compliance violations and penalties
  • Inability to meet contractual SLAs

HealaGuard's disaster recovery solutions ensure your business can maintain operations and recover quickly from any disruption.

Technical Questions

What is real-time data replication? ▼

Real-time data replication is the continuous synchronization of data from your primary systems to secondary backup locations. Unlike traditional backups that run on a schedule (hourly, daily), real-time replication captures every change as it happens.

This provides several critical benefits:

  • Near-zero Recovery Point Objective (RPO) - minimal to no data loss
  • Instant failover capabilities when needed
  • Always up-to-date backup copies
  • Protection against ransomware and corruption

HealaGuard's replication technology works at the block or byte level, ensuring efficiency and accuracy across all supported platforms.

What is RPO and RTO? ▼

RPO (Recovery Point Objective) is the maximum amount of data you can afford to lose, measured in time. For example, an RPO of 15 minutes means you can lose up to 15 minutes of data in a disaster.

RTO (Recovery Time Objective) is the maximum amount of time it takes to restore your systems after a disaster. For example, an RTO of 1 hour means your systems must be operational within 60 minutes.

HealaGuard delivers:

  • RPO: Near-zero with real-time replication
  • RTO: Minutes with automated failover

We help you define appropriate RPO/RTO targets based on your business requirements and budget.

How does automated failover work? ▼

Our automated failover system continuously monitors your primary infrastructure for issues. When it detects a failure that meets your configured thresholds, it automatically:

  • Verifies the failure is genuine (not a false alarm)
  • Initiates failover to your backup systems
  • Redirects traffic and updates DNS if needed
  • Notifies your team of the failover event
  • Monitors backup systems for proper operation

You can choose between fully automatic failover or manual approval, depending on your preferences. Once your primary systems are restored, we can fail back automatically or on command.
